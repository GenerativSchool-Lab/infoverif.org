## Category Descriptions

### I_p — Intensité persuasive (Persuasive Intensity)
Techniques exploiting emotions, moral outrage, and psychological triggers to bypass rational evaluation.

**English equivalents**: Propaganda intensity, Emotional manipulation, Persuasive pressure

### N_s — Narratif spéculatif (Speculative Narrative)
Techniques signaling conspiratorial thinking, institutional distrust, and unfalsifiable claims.

**English equivalents**: Conspiratorial signals, Speculative framing, Distrust narratives

### F_f — Fiabilité factuelle (Factual Reliability)
Techniques involving logical fallacies, evidence distortion, and factual misrepresentation.

**English equivalents**: Factual reliability, Logical fallacies, Evidence manipulation

---

## Family Descriptions

### Émotion (Emotion)
Techniques that exploit emotional responses to circumvent rational analysis.

**Key techniques**: Fear appeals, anger triggers, compassion manipulation, artificial urgency

### Simplification (Simplification)
Techniques that reduce complexity into false binaries and oversimplified narratives.

**Key techniques**: Binary framing, false dilemmas, stereotyping, caricature

### Discrédit (Discrediting)
Techniques that attack sources, credibility, and character rather than arguments.

**Key techniques**: Ad hominem, guilt by association, source dismissal, mockery

### Diversion (Diversion)
Techniques that deflect attention from the main issue through distraction and misdirection.

**Key techniques**: Whataboutism, red herrings, conspiracy theories, false equivalences

### Décontextualisation (Decontextualization)
Techniques that remove or distort context to mislead.

**Key techniques**: Truncated quotes, misleading statistics, decontextualized data, false attribution

### Rhétorique (Rhetoric)
Advanced rhetorical and persuasive techniques, including logical fallacies and cognitive biases.

**Key techniques**: Repetition, loaded language, false compromise, cognitive exploitation
